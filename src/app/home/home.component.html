Reserve a Day-Pass

The BC Parks day use pilot project is back for the 2021 season to help manage visitation levels at five of our most popular day use parks.

Visitors are now required to reserve a day pass to visit participating parks. Depending on the park, a day-use pass may not be required for all trails. If the park is open and the trail or trailhead isn’t listed, you do not need to reserve a day pass. Check the park webpage at www.bcparks.ca for details on where you need a day-use pass.

Important booking information:
  • Bookings can be made starting at 7:00am, one day in advance of your planned visit. Same day passes will be available as capacity allows.
  • Passes cannot be transferred to another date, time, or location and your pass cannot be shared beyond the group reserved.
Pass types:
  • Vehicle pass – 1 pass required per vehicle. Maximum of 8 persons (adult or child) per vehicle per day.
  • Trail pass – 1 pass required per person. Maximum of 4 passes per account per day (note: those 18 and under do not require a pass when accompanied by a parent or guardian).
When visiting the great outdoors make sure to:
  • Plan ahead. Download and use the AdventureSmart trip planning app.
  • Do your research and pack the essentials. Nature is unpredictable, so it’s important to be prepared for anything.
  • Make a trip plan and tell someone where you are going and when you expect to be back.
  • Pack out all garbage, recyclables, and organics. Organic matter can attract wildlife.
  • Always travel within your abilities and prepare for your trip. Follow in-park signage and have your pass accessible while in the park and on the trail.

For more information on the day pass pilot program, including what to expect in park, please visit us at https://bcparks.ca/reserve/day-use/.
